Supporting NJ Schools and Students Through Grief and Loss
New Jersey's Commitment to Becoming Grief-Informed
In January 2024, New Jersey enacted P.L.2023, c.201 — requiring grief and loss education to be included in Health and Physical Education instruction for students in grades 8–12.
The NJ Grief Collaborative is here to help schools, educators, families, and communities implement this mandate with confidence, compassion, and clarity.

WHY THIS MATTERS
Grief Is Not Rare —(See edit)
1 in 13 children in New Jersey will experience the death of a parent or sibling by the time they reach 18 years of age   Many more experience other significant losses such as divorce, incarceration, illness, community violence, displacement, natural disasters or identity-based loss.
Grief does not stay at home.  It shows up in classrooms, hallways, attendance records, behaviors, and academic performance.
When we equip students with knowledge,  proper language, coping tools, and trusted adults, we strengthen not only individual resilience — but entire school communities.

ABOUT THE MANDATE
What Is P.L.2023, c.201?
P.L.2023, c.201 requires that grief and loss education be included within New Jersey's Health and Physical Education standards for grades 8–12.
This mandate:
	•	Normalizes grief as a human experience
	•	Provides students with developmentally appropriate coping tools
	•	Expands the conversation to include many forms of loss
	•	Encourages schools to become grief-informed — not grief-centered not sure they will know the difference between the terminology
This mandate does not require personal disclosure, therapy, or teachers to become counselors. It ensures students receive education that prepares them for real life.

WHO WE ARE

Together, we support schools and communities in implementing grief education in ways that are developmentally appropriate, trauma-informed, and grounded in best practice.
Our organizations collaborate on training, materials, consultation, and community response to ensure schools feel supported — not overwhelmed.

WHAT GRIEF-INFORMED SCHOOLS LOOK LIKE
A grief-informed school:
	•	Recognizes that loss impacts learning and creates systems that support students throughout their school experience
	•	Prepares educators with professional development to support students and colleagues
	•	Integrates grief education into the school day thoughtfully
	•	Creates space for questions without requiring disclosure
	•	Provides pathways to additional support when needed

3. About the Grief Education Mandate (P.L.2023, c.201)
Purpose: Clearly explain the legislation in accessible, non-technical language.
Sections:
	•	What Is P.L.2023, c.201?
	•	In January 2024, Governor Phil Murphy signed into law a mandate requiring grief and loss education to be included within Health and Physical Education standards for grades 8–12 in all New Jersey public schools.
	•	Who It Impacts
	•	Families: Awareness of when and how grief education is addressed and what content is used
	•	Educators: Access to trusted resources and support from NJ grief organizations
	•	Schools: Understanding that educators teaching grief and loss should receive professional development prior to instruction
	•	What It Is / What It Is Not
	•	This mandate is NOT:
	•	Telling students how to grieve
	•	Providing grief support groups in schools
	•	Asking students to disclose personal losses
	•	Expecting teachers to act as counselors
	•	This mandate IS:
	•	A way to normalize grief as a human experience
	•	An opportunity to equip students and families with coping tools and resources
	•	A broader conversation about grief related to many forms of loss, not only death
	•	Status with the NJ Department of Education
	•	The DOE's Physical Education and Health Standards Review Committee is drafting standards for adoption in 2027
	•	Frequently Asked Questions
	•	Does this need to be taught every year?
	•	Must this be taught exclusively in Health or PE?
	•	Where can educators access professional development?
	•	Where can schools find sample lesson plans?
	•	Can outside presenters or speakers be used?
